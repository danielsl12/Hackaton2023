General note
The information provided in the manual is not a substitute for advice or the basis for a medical diagnosis. For more detailed information about the drugs please refer to the Consumer Bulletin, as approved by the Ministry of Health. On any further questions it is necessary to consult with the attending physician or pharmacist.
What is it for?
• Muphyrosine (also sometimes written mopyrocin) is an external use only antibiotic ointment that is used to treat skin infections such as infection of the hair follicles that creates purulent pimples, impetigo (a type of infectious skin infection) and recurrent purulent wounds on the skin.
• Mophyrosine is associated with a skin ointment with the trade names Bactrovan and Mopyrosine Teva. The ointment for the skin should not be applied to the nose or eyes.
• Mophyrosine is also associated with ointment for the nose.
• The drug is prescribed only by a doctor's prescription. It is forbidden to use it without consulting a doctor. Using an antibiotic ointment not under the orders of a doctor can cause the development of resistant bacteria and may result in the skin not being treated properly.
Is it possible to apply mopyrosine on an open wound?
Used on extensive skin areas where the skin is damaged or on open wounds, the inactive substance in the ointment may be absorbed into the bloodstream. Therefore, the use of Mopyrosine ointment on an open wound will be done only if the doctor confirms it. This guidance is especially important when it comes to patients with kidney failure.
Is mopyrosine suitable for the treatment of fungus?
Mopyrosine is an antibiotic drug that is intended for the treatment of bacterial skin infections. It is not suitable for the treatment of skin fungus.
Is Muphyrosine ointment also suitable for children?
The ointment is suitable for newborns and children - subject to doctor's approval - but it is not suitable for the treatment of diaper rash or minor irritation in the buttocks area.
In how long does the drug begin to affect?
The onset of the effect of the drug varies depending on the severity of the skin infection. The doctor can assess - according to the severity of the skin infection - when improvement is expected to be felt.
What is the duration of the effect of the drug?
• The drug has an effect as long as it is used.
• The doctor determines the duration of treatment.
• The maximum duration of treatment with ointment is 10 days.

 What is the common dosage?
 Apply a little ointment 2 to 3 times a day for a maximum of 10 days. The ointment can be covered with a band-aid or bandage, if the doctor recommends it.
 Instructions for use
 • Before using the drug, wash your hands thoroughly with water and soap.
 • Apply a small amount of the ointment to the affected skin 2 to 3 times a day according to the doctor's instructions.
 • After applying the ointment, close the tube with a cork, and then thoroughly wash your hands with soap and water.
 • It is not necessary to put a band-aid or bandage on the treated area, unless the doctor has specifically ordered it to do so.
 Should I take with food or on an empty stomach?
 Since this is an ointment to be applied topically, food has no effect on it.
 How to store the drug?
 • Keep the drug at a temperature of no higher than 25 °C, out of reach of children.
 • After opening the tube for the first time, the ointment can be used for 6 months, but not later than its expiration date.

 Note for Pregnancy and Breastfeeding sections
 The information presented in the manual regarding pregnancy and lactation is not personalized information but is based on general client information from evidence-based medical repositories and is not a substitute for consulting with a physician or specialist or with a teratologic center.
 Is the drug allowed in pregnancy?
 The ointment is intended for external use only (on the skin) and is practically not absorbed. It can be used during pregnancy - but only on the orders of a doctor.
 Is the drug allowed in lactation?
 • The ointment is intended for external use only (on the skin) and is practically not absorbed. It can be used during the lactation period - but only on the orders of a doctor.
 • If the ointment has been given to treat cracked nipples, rinse them thoroughly before each lactation to remove the remnants of the ointment from them.
 Is it allowed to drink alcohol when treated with the drug?
 Drinking alcohol should not affect the effectiveness of the ointment.
 Is it allowed to drive when treated with the drug?
 The ointment should not affect the ability to drive.
 When is it forbidden to use?
 • If there is hypersensitivity to one or more of the components of the drug.
 • The drug is intended for external use only; it is forbidden to swallow it.
 • It is forbidden to smear the family in the eyes, inside the nose or around the intravenal cannula.
 Important warnings
 • Medical history. Before starting to use the drug, it is necessary to report to the attending physician about the medical history: present and past medical conditions and diseases. It is also necessary to report to the attending physician about any additional medications that are taken, including dietary supplements and medicinal herbs.
 • Hypersensitivity. The use of the drug can cause hypersensitivity to the skin. Detailed information on the symptoms of hypersensitivity in the skin is presented in the section “What are the possible side effects”.
 • Pollution resistant. Prolonged or frequent use of the ointment can cause the development of a skin resistant infection or fungal infection. Prolonged or frequent use of the ointment should be avoided. The maximum duration of treatment is usually up to 10 days.
 • Incompletion Treatment. It is necessary to strictly observe the duration of treatment that the doctor recommends. Stopping treatment too early can cause the return of the infection.
 • How to use. The ointment is intended for external use (on the skin) only. It should not be applied to the eyes, nose, or around the into-venous cannula.
 • Renal insufficiency. If you have kidney problems or kidney failure, this should be reported to the attending physician. In case of kidney failure - especially if using the ointment on large areas of open wounds or damaged skin - the doctor will decide whether mopyrosine can be used or other treatment should be assisted. It is necessary to strictly follow the instructions of the attending physician.
 • Polyethylene glycol. The ointment contains the substance polyethylene glycol. This substance may be absorbed and accumulated in the bloodstream in cases when the ointment is applied to open wounds and extensive areas or if you have kidney failure. It is necessary to consult with the attending physician.
 What are the possible side effects?
 Side effects that require contacting the attending physician
 Most of these side effects are not common and even rare, but can be dangerous:
 • Side effects of hypersensitivity such as rash, swelling, redness, itching, appearance of blisters on the skin, flaking skin, shortness of breath, wheezing in breathing, difficulty breathing, tightness in the chest or throat, swelling of one or more of the following organs: mouth, face, lips, tongue, eyelids. To these symptoms may accompany high fever.
 • Inflammation of the large intestine that causes acute diarrhea and abdominal pain.
 Common side effects
 Burning or burning sensation in the place where the ointment is applied.
 Uncommon side effects
 Itching, redness, tingling or dryness in the place where the ointment was applied; rash, allergy, itching, redness or pain elsewhere in the skin.
 Rare side effects
 Hypersensitivity to the drug, swollen face, difficulty breathing, inflammation of the large intestine.
 Drug Interreactions
 • Before starting to use the drug, report to your doctor about any other medications that are taking or should be taking including dietary supplements and medicinal herbs.
 • It is forbidden to use this ointment together with other preparations for the skin (ointments, creams, tinctures and the like). Such a combination may reduce the effectiveness of the ointment.
