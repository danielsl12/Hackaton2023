 What is it for?

 Diclofenac is a drug belonging to the family of drugs known as “non-steroidal anti-inflammatory” (NSAID). It reduces inflammatory processes and relieves pain.

 Diclofenac is used in Israel with some preparations for internal use (including tablets, suppositories and injections), eye drops and preparations for external use. The information provided here refers to preparations for external use.

 Diclofenac for external use is used to locally relieve pain and inflammations (such as back pain or muscle pain) that may be caused by overexertion, local trauma, inflammation, and sports injuries.

 Voltaren series for outdoor use
 Voltaren is equated - among other things - with a gel for external use (sometimes mistakenly called “Voltaren Ointment” or “Voltaren Cream”, although it is a preparation for external use in the texture of a gel). The gel is paired with two different strengths:

 Voltaren emulgel 1%.

 Voltaren Forte Emulgel 2%.

 Voltaren emulgel preparations are intended for adults and adolescents from 12 years and more.

 Diclofenac is also associated with patches and spray.

 Dicloplast patch is used to relieve inflammation and local pain (such as back pain or muscle and joint pain). It is intended for those aged 16 years and more.

 The Suisse Relif spray is also intended to relieve inflammation and local pain. The spray sprinkles gel with a strength of 4% and is intended for those aged 15 years and more.
 In how long does the drug begin to affect?

 The drug begins to affect a few minutes after being applied to the skin or attached to it with a patch.
 What is the duration of the effect of the drug?

 The drug for external use affects for 8 to 12 hours.

 The duration of use is limited to a few days - depending on the type of medication. In the consumer bulletin of the various preparations, the maximum period of time for which each of the preparations can be used is indicated. It is necessary to strictly follow the instructions provided in the leaflet.

 If there is no relief following the use of local preparations, contact the attending physician.
 How the drug is administered:

 gel
 Is a prescription required?

 not

What is the common dosage?

Apply the gel 3 or 4 times a day.

Instructions for use

• Apply the gel to the painful area and massage it with circular motions until complete absorption is achieved.

• The gel may stain clothes. It is necessary to dress only after the gel is completely absorbed, and the smeared skin has dried.

• After application, rinse your palms thoroughly with water and soap to remove the remnants of the gel - unless the intention was in the first place to apply the remedy to the palms.

• The gel contains alcohol, so it must not be close to a source of fire or heat, and it is forbidden to smoke before it is completely absorbed into the skin.

• It is forbidden to apply the gel together with other skin preparations.

Should I take with food or on an empty stomach?

It is a gel for external application. Food has absolutely no effect on him.

How to store the drug?

• Keep the gel at a temperature not exceeding 30° C.

• It is forbidden to use Suisse Relif Gel Spray in half a year from its opening.

Note for Pregnancy and Breastfeeding sections

The information presented in the manual regarding pregnancy and lactation is not personalized information but is based on general client information from evidence-based medical repositories and is not a substitute for consulting with a physician or specialist or with a teratologic center.
Is the drug allowed in pregnancy?

It is forbidden to use the gel in the last three months of pregnancy.
Is the drug allowed in lactation?

Voltaren gel can be used during the lactation period, if the doctor confirms it. However, it is forbidden to apply the gel to the breasts or chest.

It is necessary to prevent any contact of the mammal baby with the gel. Therefore, it is recommended to apply the gel after breastfeeding, and if it is applied to the torso, for example, the arms and hands, the area should be washed before breastfeeding.
Is it allowed to drink alcohol when treated with the drug?

Drinking alcohol should not affect the effectiveness of the drug.
Is it allowed to drive when treated with the drug?

The use of the gel should not affect driving capacity.
When is it forbidden to use?

• If there is hypersensitivity to one or more of the components of the preparation.

• If there is hypersensitivity to any drug from the family of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs such as aspirin and ibuprofen. This hypersensitivity can be expressed in an asthma attack (including symptoms such as shortness of breath and wheezing in the respiratory system), rash, and allergic rhinitis.

• It is forbidden to apply the gel to injured, open or irritated skin.

• Before the age of 12 or 15 years - depending on the type of commercial preparation.

Important warnings

• Before starting to use the drug, it is necessary to report to the attending physician about the medical history, including past and present diseases - especially asthma, liver and kidney dysfunction, heart disease, stomach ulcers, bleeding from the gastrointestinal tract.

• It is also necessary to report to the attending physician - even before starting to use the medicament - about any additional medication used, including dietary supplements.

• It is forbidden to apply the gel to injured or damaged skin.

• It is forbidden to apply the gel near the eyes or near mucous tissues such as the mouth and nose. Moreover, the medicament is not intended for application to the face.

• The gel contains alcohol, so it must not be exposed to any source of fire (including a cigarette) until the complete drying of the smeared area.

• It is not recommended to use the gel on extensive skin areas without obtaining permission from the attending physician.

• It is forbidden to use the drug for a long period without consulting a doctor. Prolonged pain or inflammation requires medical clarification.

• When treated with this gel, avoid exposure to the sun. It is also necessary to avoid exposure to tanning lamps and other sources of radiation.

• If a skin rash develops, the use of the gel should be discontinued.

• It is forbidden to use the gel before and after bypass surgery.

What are the possible side effects?

Side effects that require you to see a doctor immediately
• Skin allergy symptoms such as rash, sores, peeling or swelling. To these symptoms may accompany fever.

• Symptoms of angioedema (type of allergy): swelling in one or more of the following organs: the face, Cheeks, eyelids, lips, tongue, throat. These symptoms may be accompanied by shortness of breath and a feeling of suffocation.

• Symptoms of asthma such as shortness of breath and wheezing in breathing.

• Diffuse or localized rash on the skin.

• Pressure or chest pain.

• Weakness on one side of the body to which are accompanied by difficulty pronouncing words and changes in facial appearance.

• Swelling of the feet or of the ankles.

• Increase in blood pressure.

• Increased or rapid pulse.

• flu-like symptoms.

Common side effects
Itching, redness, rash, sharp pain in the skin.

Rare side effects
sensitivity to the sun. Sun exposure of gel-treated skin can cause redness, burns, swelling, and the appearance of blisters on the skin. In such cases, stop the use of the gel and contact the attending physician.

Drug Interreactions

• Before starting use of the gel (which we recall is intended for external application) report to your doctor or pharmacist about any other medication that is taking or should be taking, including dietary supplements.

Since the absorption of the gel is minimal, and the effect of the drug is local, in most cases the doctor or pharmacist will confirm the combination with the other drugs, but monitoring of the side effects of the same drugs may be required.

• These are the drugs that may be affected by diclofenac gel: other drugs from the family of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs such as ibuprofen and aspirin, methotrexate, lithium, cyclosporin, anticoagulants such as Warfarin and ACE inhibitors such as ramipril.

• Diclofenac gel should not be used simultaneously and on the same piece of skin along with other preparations intended for application to the skin such as tinctures, creams and ointments.
